I had the chance to collaborate in a small team to build a single, high-quality game. The game was developed within an agile-led development structure, drawing on the experience gained in other modules to plan and control my progress.

Level Design Process — Level 01 — Sketch

I had the opportunity to work with a team in professional conditions. We were tasked to design and develop a 2d platformer and provide a pitch document, game design document, and promotion and supporting plan. My role in this project was that of a level designer. I was responsible for designing and creating levels that would fit the theme and dynamic of the game.

Level Design Process — Level 01 — Graybox

This is the first version of the first level, which is inspired by Ori and the Blind Forrest. The first level introduces the players to the game mechanics and teaches them how they can be used.

Level Design — Level 01

The most challenging aspect of this process was the sizing of the platforms and introducing puzzle areas. A setback that happened during this process was that when the assets were chosen for this level, the size was not right, meaning the graybox was too small for the assets, therefore, I had to re-design the whole level from 0 so that the size would be right.

Level Design Process — Level 02 — Sketch

This first version of the second level was inspired form Ori and the Blind Forrest and Celleste. This level features more environmental hazards and more synchronized jumps, making the second level more challenging than the first one which thought the player how to maneuver the game.

Level Design Process — Level 02 — Graybox

The same story happened with the second-level graybox. The size was just not right, therefore, the level had to be re-designed so that the assets would be the right size, and the look and feel of the game would be improved.
