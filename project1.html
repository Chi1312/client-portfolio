Level Design

Deathmatch Level Design graybox inspired from Gears of War

This was a project for one of my courses. I was assigned to create and design a graybox level for a multiplayer shooter game, which would illustrate risk and reward areas, various weapon placements, elevation and cover areas.

First Sketch — Deathmatch

As a first take on the task, I designed a Deathmatch level inspired from Halo. Using stairs and corridors to create elevation and diversity in the environment tured out to be a bit confusing both for designing a graybox and for the items placement.

Second Sketch — Capture the flag

The second attempt on the task was represented by a Catch the flag level inspired by Halo 2. It was easier to sketch and represent the walk paths, corridors, and item placing. Although, the middle corridor seemed too clustered, in comparison with the bottom corridor which was almost empty.

Final Sketch — Deathmatch

The third version of the prototype was a deathmatch level inspired by Gears of War. This sketch is more detailed and it is easier to follow than the first ones. This sketch shows the items' placement, risk and reward areas, and all the possible paths players can take. Cover areas are also illustrated.
