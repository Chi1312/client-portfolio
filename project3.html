Pitch Card for a proposed game

I created a game design document for a rogue-like dungeon crawler mobile game. The document includes the design of a core loop, win/lose conditions, game mechanics, level progression, and stats improvement. The following items represent the steps taken in the process of designing a prototype for a game.

The core loop

The player enters a dungeon and kills all the monsters inside each room. Killing monsters rewards the player with exp points; Leveling up unlocks new attacks and boosts stats. After killing the enemies and the boss, the player must find a hidden room behind a false wall, which will appear only after the boss is defeated, solve the puzzle from the hidden room to gain a cue used to complete the map to the Time Capsule, and the dungeon is complete.

The gameplay screen

The player-character and enemies feature a health bar to keep track of combat progress. The left joystick is for movement and the right one is for aiming and shooting enemies. I thought that implementing a roaming glowing pouch which if caught may bring a lot of rare items.

Item inventory

This is a sketch of what the shop/inventory window would look like. The player must choose a shield and a weapon to fight with. Each item has a name, a rarity level, special stats, and a unique power.

Tutorial Screen

One of the first scenes that must be created is the tutorial scene, which should be kept brief and simple, teaching the player the basics of the game, in this case, the tutorial scene is teaching players how to move, aim and shoot.

Dungeon Map

At first, when designing the map, I was inspired by Ori and the Blind Forest, Soul Knight, and Diablo. My vision featured many rooms, some hidden ones, and tight corridors. Hidden rooms can be found by looking for key items or solving puzzles throughout the level.
